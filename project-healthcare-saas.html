Our client needed a smarter way to coordinate care between eye care organizations and their physicians. I joined as Senior Product Designer for a short-term, high-intensity engagement focused on redesigning key features of their SaaS platform — making patient management more intuitive and clinically useful.

The Problem

No Centralized Patient View

Organizations and physicians worked in silos, with no unified visibility into patient status. Coordination depended on manual, offline communication between teams.

Broken Communication Workflows

The platform lacked structured flows for organizations and physicians to communicate efficiently. Critical information fell through the gaps, impacting the quality of care.

Low Clinical Usability

The interface didn't match the speed and clarity that clinical staff needed. Navigation was complex, key actions were buried, and the cognitive load was too high for a fast-paced healthcare environment.

Process

Focused Discovery

Despite the tight timeline, I ran a structured discovery phase — reviewing existing flows, mapping the highest-friction points with the clinical team, and aligning on scope for the sprint.

Core UX Redesign

Redesigned the main patient management and physician collaboration screens, simplifying navigation and reducing the number of steps required for the most common clinical tasks.

Unified CRM Interface

Worked on the central CRM view, replacing fragmented data displays with a single screen connecting organizations, physicians, and patient information in a readable, actionable format.

Patient-Centric Flows

Ensured every interaction in the platform served the needs of the individual patient — from scheduling and communication to data visibility and follow-up tracking.

Key Insight

In healthcare SaaS, simplicity is a clinical requirement. Every extra click, every ambiguous label, every redundant screen costs time that clinicians don't have. The redesign prioritized speed and clarity above all else.

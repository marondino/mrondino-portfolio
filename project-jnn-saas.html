JNN Group needed to build a SaaS platform — "Quantome" — to streamline cybersecurity talent acquisition using AI. I joined in a hybrid role as both Product Manager and Product Designer, owning the product end-to-end from strategy and discovery through design and delivery.

The Problem

Talent Scarcity in Cybersecurity

Demand for qualified cybersecurity professionals far exceeded supply. Companies were spending months on hiring processes with no guarantee of quality at the end.

Slow, Manual Screening

Existing hiring flows relied on manual resume reviews and unstructured interviews — time-consuming, inconsistent, and expensive at scale.

No Trust or Verification Layer

Companies had no reliable way to verify that candidates were who they claimed to be. This created risk and eroded confidence in the hiring process.

High Cost Per Hire

Prolonged processes drained budgets that companies needed to invest in actual security infrastructure.

Process

As Product Manager

Defined and prioritized the product backlog based on business goals and user research. Tracked risks and dependencies across the full delivery cycle. Planned and managed release milestones end-to-end. Facilitated a Design Sprint to align the team and validate the core product concept early.

As Product Designer

Led user research to understand the cybersecurity hiring market and key pain points on both sides — companies and candidates. Designed end-to-end UX flows, wireframes, prototypes, and high-fidelity screens across all core features.

AI-Powered Screening

Designed the UX for an AI layer that analyzed resumes and screening responses, surfacing only the most qualified candidates — dramatically reducing manual review time.

ID Verification Flow

Built a rigorous identity verification process into the candidate journey, giving companies confidence in who they were engaging with before investing in interviews.

Talent Profiles & Matching Interface

Designed structured, scannable talent profiles optimized for security roles, and a matching interface allowing companies to filter by skill set, certifications, seniority, and availability.

Key Insight

Holding both PM and Design roles revealed how much friction typically lives at the handoff between strategy and execution. Owning both eliminated that gap entirely — decisions were faster, priorities were clearer, and the team moved with significantly less ambiguity.
